For Peterson, a natural doorway is the bounded prefix consisting of:

* `flag[i] = true`
* `victim = i`

and the waiting section is the `while (flag[j] && victim == i) {}` loop.

With that doorway, Peterson gives **1-bounded waiting** (`r = 1`). If thread A finishes its doorway before thread B starts its doorway, B may overtake A at most once, but not twice. After one critical-section entry by B, Peterson forces B to wait while A is still interested.
