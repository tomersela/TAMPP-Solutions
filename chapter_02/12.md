Consider `n = 4`, with threads `A, B, C, D`, and levels `1, 2, 3`.

A simple execution is:

1. `A` is delayed in the wait at level `1`.
2. `B` is delayed in the wait at level `2`.
3. `C` is delayed in the wait at level `3`.
4. `D` passes level `3`, enters the critical section, and unlocks.
5. Then `C` enters the critical section and unlocks.
6. Then `B` enters the critical section and unlocks.

At this point, `A` is no longer blocked at level `1`, so `A` runs and advances to level `2`.

7. Before `A` can continue further, schedule `B`, `C`, and `D` again.
8. They start a new lock attempt and climb back above `A`.
9. One of them enters the critical section before `A`.

So `A` has already advanced to the next level, and is still overtaken.

The same pattern can be repeated: every time `A` advances one level, pause it, let the other threads start again, rebuild the same situation, and let one of them enter before `A`. Therefore, for any finite number `r`, there is an execution in which `A` is overtaken more than `r` times before it enters the critical section. Hence, the Filter lock does not satisfy bounded waiting.
