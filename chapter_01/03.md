If Bob cannot observe Alice's can, any scheme where he must read her side to know it is safe to produce again is impossible. That matches interrupt-style bits: the device can signal the CPU, but it cannot rely on reading the same state the handler cleared. Use two cans so each party only waits on what they can see: Bob gets a separate ack on his own sill; Alice still watches her food-available can.

Two cans, each observable by whoever must react:

| Can | Location | Who knocks (writes / lowers) | Who observes (reads) |
|-----|----------|------------------------------|----------------------|
| Food-available | Alice's windowsill | Bob | Alice |
| Empty-yard | Bob's windowsill | Alice | Bob |

Bob never has to see Alice's can to know Alice reset it; he only needs Alice to knock his empty-yard can when she is done. Alice never has to infer Bob's private state except by her own can.

**Initial state.** Empty-yard can on Bob's side is down (Bob may place food; no portion is waiting to be consumed). Food-available can on Alice's side is up.

### Protocol

**Bob (producer).**

1. Wait until the empty-yard can on his sill is down.
2. Enter the yard and place food.
3. Leave the yard.
4. Reset the empty-yard can to up.
5. Pull the string that knocks down the food-available can on Alice's side.

**Alice (consumer).**

1. Wait until the food-available can on her sill is down.
2. Release the pets into the yard.
3. When the pets return, check that the food is gone.
4. Reset the food-available can to up.
5. Knock down Bob's empty-yard can: ack that this round is consumed and Bob may deliver the next batch.

### Why it works

This is a two-way handshake:

- Bob does not place new food until Alice signals on hardware Bob can see (his empty-yard can).
- Alice does not release the pets until Bob signals on hardware Alice can see (her food-available can).
- Pets never enter unless food was signaled; Bob does not refill while an old batch is still pending.
- Bob leaves the yard before knocking Alice's can, so Bob and the pets are never in the yard together.

**Interrupt analogy.** Bob raises "food ready" toward Alice (like asserting an IRQ). Alice clears that side when she finishes and pulses a separate "I'm ready for more" to Bob (like EOI or a distinct status/ack line the producer monitors), without either side needing to read the other's non-observable register.
