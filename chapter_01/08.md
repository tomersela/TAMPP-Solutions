The uniprocessor executes **5 zillion instructions per second**.

The multiprocessor executes **10 × 1 = 10 zillion instructions per second** on perfectly parallel work.

So relative to the uniprocessor, the multiprocessor gives only a **2x** speedup on the parallelizable part:

$$
10 / 5 = 2
$$

If \(p\) is the fraction of the program that can be parallelized, then the overall speedup of the multiprocessor relative to the uniprocessor is:

$$
S = \frac{1}{(1-p) + \frac{p}{2}}
$$

Therefore:

- if \(p\) is small, buy the **faster uniprocessor**
- if \(p\) is large, buy the **ten-processor multiprocessor**

So the decision depends on the application’s **parallelizable fraction**.
