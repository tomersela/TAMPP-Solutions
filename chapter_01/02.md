
A **safety** property says "a bad thing never happens." If it's violated, you can point to a specific moment where things went wrong, and no future execution can undo it.

A **liveness** property says "a good thing eventually happens." It can't be violated in finite time - you can only confirm a violation by observing an infinite execution where the good thing never shows up.



**1. Patrons are served in the order they arrive.**

**Safety.** The bad thing of interest is a patron who arrived first not being served first. If patron A arrives before patron B but A is not served before B, the property is violated at that moment, and nothing later can fix it.



**2. What goes up must come down.**

**Liveness.** The good thing of interest is that something that went up eventually comes down. At any finite point you can't say it's violated - maybe it just hasn't come down *yet*.



**3. If two or more processes are waiting to enter their critical sections, at least one succeeds.**

**Liveness.** The good thing of interest is that at least one waiting process eventually enters its critical section. You can't point to a single moment where this fails - you'd have to observe the processes waiting forever with none ever getting in.



**4. If an interrupt occurs, then a message is printed within one second.**

**Safety.** The bad thing of interest is an interrupt happening with no message printed within one second. There's a concrete deadline: once one second passes after the interrupt without a message, the property is violated and nothing can undo it.



**5. If an interrupt occurs, then a message is printed.**

**Liveness.** The good thing of interest is that a message is eventually printed after an interrupt. At any finite point you can't confirm a violation - the message could still be printed in the future. There's no deadline.



**6. The cost of living never decreases.**

**Safety.** The bad thing of interest is the cost of living decreasing. If it drops at any point, the property is violated at that moment.



**7. Two things are certain: death and taxes.**

**Liveness.** The good thing of interest is that death and taxes eventually happen. At any finite point in time, you can't say they've been violated - they might still occur.



**8. You can always tell a Harvard man.**

**Safety.** The bad thing of interest is encountering a Harvard man you can't tell. If at any point you meet one and can't identify them, the property is violated.
