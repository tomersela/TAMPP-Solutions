A **safety** property says "a bad thing never happens." If it's violated, you can point to a specific moment where things went wrong, and no future execution can undo it.

A **liveness** property says "a good thing eventually happens." It can't be violated in finite time - you can only confirm a violation by observing an infinite execution where the good thing never shows up.


**1. Patrons are served in the order they arrive.**

**Safety.** The bad thing of interest is a patron who arrived first not being served first. If patron A arrives before patron B but A is not served before B, the property is violated at that moment, and nothing later can fix it.


**2. Anything that can go wrong, will go wrong.**

**Liveness.** The thing of interest is that something that can go wrong eventually does go wrong. You cannot show a violation at any finite instant; the mishap might still occur later. (Murphy's law is an "eventually" claim, even though the event is bad, not good.)


**3. No one wants to die.**

**Safety.** Read as a property we would like the world to satisfy: the bad thing of interest is death (or someone being dead). The sentence states a universal wish that that bad thing never happen. Formalizing loosely: we care about avoiding the bad outcome; that is the safety pattern. (The wording is psychological, not a program spec.)


**4. Two things are certain: death and taxes.**

**Liveness.** The good thing of interest is that death and paying taxes eventually occur. At any finite time you cannot say the claim has failed; they might still happen later.


**5. As soon as one is born, one starts dying.**

**Safety.** It ties two events, birth and the start of dying, with no gap or wrong order. The bad thing of interest is a violation of that linkage: for example dying (or starting to die) before being born, or being born without the "dying has started" condition when the rule says it must hold immediately. Any such inconsistency is visible at some moment; it is not something you only discover by waiting forever. Same pattern as ordering constraints in synchronization: a bad combined state or bad ordering never happens.


**6. If an interrupt occurs, then a message is printed within one second.**

**Safety.** The bad thing of interest is an interrupt happening with no message printed within one second. There is a hard deadline: once one second passes after the interrupt without a message, the property is violated and nothing can undo it.


**7. If an interrupt occurs, then a message is printed.**

**Liveness.** The good thing of interest is that a message is eventually printed after an interrupt. At any finite point you cannot confirm a violation; the message could still be printed later. There is no deadline.


**8. I will finish what Darth Vader has started.**

**Liveness.** The good thing of interest is that the work is eventually finished (the speaker's promise is kept). You only know it failed if you observe an execution where that completion never arrives.


**9. The cost of living never decreases.**

**Safety.** The bad thing of interest is the cost of living decreasing. If it drops at any point, the property is violated at that moment.


**10. You can always tell a Harvard man.**

**Safety.** "Always" means whenever the situation applies (he is a Harvard man), you can tell, not "keep waiting and eventually you'll know." The bad thing of interest is meeting a Harvard man you cannot tell; that falsifies the claim at that moment. A liveness reading would need different wording (for example "eventually you'll be able to tell"), which this sentence does not say.
