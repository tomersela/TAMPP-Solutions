Amdahl’s Law gives:

$$
S_n = \frac{1}{(1-p) + \frac{p}{n}}
$$

For two processors:

$$
S_2 = \frac{1}{(1-p) + \frac{p}{2}}
$$

Simplify the denominator:

$$
(1-p) + \frac{p}{2} = 1 - \frac{p}{2}
$$

So:

$$
S_2 = \frac{1}{1 - \frac{p}{2}}
$$

Now solve for $p$:

$$
S_2\left(1 - \frac{p}{2}\right) = 1
$$

$$
S_2 - \frac{S_2p}{2} = 1
$$

$$
S_2 - 1 = \frac{S_2p}{2}
$$

$$
p = \frac{2(S_2 - 1)}{S_2}
$$

Therefore:

$$
p = 2 - \frac{2}{S_2}
$$

Substitute this into the expression for $S_n$:

$$
S_n = \frac{1}{(1-p) + \frac{p}{n}}
$$

$$
S_n = \frac{1}{\left(1 - \left(2 - \frac{2}{S_2}\right)\right) + \frac{2 - \frac{2}{S_2}}{n}}
$$

$$
S_n = \frac{1}{-1 + \frac{2}{S_2} + \frac{2}{n} - \frac{2}{nS_2}}
$$

Combine the denominator into a single fraction:

$$
-1 + \frac{2}{S_2} + \frac{2}{n} - \frac{2}{nS_2} = \frac{-nS_2 + 2n + 2S_2 - 2}{nS_2}
$$

So:

$$
S_n = \frac{1}{\frac{-nS_2 + 2n + 2S_2 - 2}{nS_2}} = \frac{nS_2}{-nS_2 + 2n + 2S_2 - 2}
$$

### Final formula

$$
S_n = \frac{nS_2}{2n + 2S_2 - nS_2 - 2}
$$

An equivalent form is:

$$
S_n = \frac{nS_2}{2(n-1) + (2-n)S_2}
$$

### Intuition

Under Amdahl’s model, the entire scaling behavior is determined by a single parameter, $p$, the parallelizable fraction.

Knowing $S_2$ allows us to recover $p$, and once $p$ is known, the speedup for any number of processors is fixed by the formula above.

This is an idealized model. In real systems, speedup often does not scale this neatly because of synchronization overhead, contention, communication costs, memory effects, and load imbalance.
