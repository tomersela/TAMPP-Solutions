### Core idea

A correct strategy is to designate one prisoner as the counter.

- Every other prisoner is allowed to turn the switch on only once: the first time they enter the room and find it off.
- The counter turns the switch off whenever they see it on, and increments their count.
- Once the counter has counted all other prisoners, they can safely declare that all prisoners have visited the room at least once.

### Why it works

Each non-counter prisoner contributes at most one signal by turning the switch from off to on exactly once.

Each time the counter sees the switch on, they know that some prisoner has contributed a signal that can now be counted.

Because every prisoner is guaranteed to visit the room infinitely many times, eventually:

- each non-counter prisoner will contribute their signal
- the counter will eventually observe and count every such signal

At that point, the counter knows that everyone has visited the room at least once.
