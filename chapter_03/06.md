**Only if.** If $H$ is linearizable, fix a witness: extension $H'$ and sequential $S$ with $\mathit{complete}(H') \equiv S$ and $\rightarrow_H$ respected in $S$.

For object $x$, use the same completion on $x$-calls as in $H'$ (that is $H'|x$), and take $S|x$ = subsequence of $S$ of all $x$-operations, in $S$’s order. Then $\mathit{complete}(H'|x) \equiv S|x$, $S|x$ is sequentially legal for $x$, and if $m_0 \rightarrow_{H|x} m_1$ then $m_0 \rightarrow_H m_1$, so $m_0$ stays before $m_1$ in $S|x$. Hence $H|x$ is linearizable.
