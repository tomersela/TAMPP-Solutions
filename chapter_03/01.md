### Theorem
If for every object $x$, the projection $H|x$ is quiescent-consistent, then the full history $H$ is quiescent-consistent.


### Proof

Let $H$ be partitioned by its global quiescent cuts into blocks

$$
B_1, B_2, \dots, B_k,
$$

where each $B_i$ is the set of operations between two consecutive global quiescent periods.

For each object $x$, choose a QC serialization:

$$
S_x = S_{x,1} \cdot S_{x,2} \cdot \dots \cdot S_{x,k}.
$$

We construct a global history:

$$
S = M_1 \cdot M_2 \cdot \dots \cdot M_k,
$$

where each $M_i$ is a merge of $S_{1,i}, \dots, S_{n,i}$ preserving internal order.


## 1. Method calls should appear to happen in a one-at-a-time, sequential order.

### Lemma 1.1 - Per-block merge exists

For each block $B_i$, there exists a sequence $M_i$ that is a merge of

$$
S_{1,i}, S_{2,i}, \dots, S_{n,i},
$$

preserving the internal order of each sequence.

#### Proof

Each $S_{x,i}$ is finite. We can interleave them while preserving internal order.


### Lemma 1.2 - Each operation appears exactly once

Every operation in $H$ appears exactly once in $S$.

#### Proof

Each operation belongs to exactly one block $B_i$ and one object $x$, hence appears in exactly one $S_{x,i}$, therefore exactly once in $M_i$, and thus exactly once in $S$.


### Lemma 1.3 - Sequentiality

$S$ is a single sequential history.

#### Proof

$S$ is constructed as a concatenation of sequences:

$$
S = M_1 \cdot M_2 \cdot \dots \cdot M_k,
$$

so all operations appear in a single total order.


### Lemma 1.4 - Legality

$S$ is a legal sequential history.

#### Proof

Fix an object $x$. The projection $S|x$ is:

$$
S_x = S_{x,1} \cdot S_{x,2} \cdot \dots \cdot S_{x,k},
$$

because each merge preserves internal order.

Since $S_x$ is a legal QC serialization of $H|x$, the projection is legal.

Therefore $S$ is legal.


## 2. Method calls separated by a period of quiescence should appear to take effect in their real-time order

### Lemma 2.1 - Projection preserves quiescent cuts

Every quiescent cut in $H$ is also a quiescent cut in $H|x$ for every object $x$.

#### Proof

If some operation overlapped the cut in $H|x$, it would also overlap it in $H$, contradicting quiescence.


### Lemma 2.2 - Per-object block decomposition

For each object $x$, the QC serialization $S_x$ can be written as:

$$
S_x = S_{x,1} \cdot S_{x,2} \cdot \dots \cdot S_{x,k},
$$

where $S_{x,i}$ contains exactly the operations from $B_i$.

#### Proof

By Lemma 2.1, global quiescent cuts appear in $H|x$.

Since $S_x$ is QC, it must respect those cuts, so operations are grouped per block.


### Lemma 2.3 - Cross-object order preservation

Let $e_{1,a} \in B_i$ and $e_{2,b} \in B_j$ with $i < j$. Then in $S$:

$$
e_{1,a} \to e_{2,b}.
$$

#### Proof

$e_{1,a}$ appears in $M_i$, and $e_{2,b}$ appears in $M_j$.

Since

$$
S = M_1 \cdot M_2 \cdot \dots \cdot M_k
$$

and $i < j$, all operations in $M_i$ precede all operations in $M_j$.

Thus $e_{1,a} \to e_{2,b}$.


### Conclusion

By Section 1, $S$ is a legal sequential history.

By Section 2, $S$ preserves the order of operations separated by quiescent periods.

Therefore, $S$ is a quiescent-consistent serialization of $H$, and $H$ is quiescent-consistent.
