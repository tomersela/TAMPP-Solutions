# Fig 3.13
The history shown in Fig 3.13 is both Quiescent-consistent, Sequential-consistent, and Linearizable:

**Quiescent-consistency** - $<r:read(1), A>$, $<r:write(1), B>$, $<r:write(2), C>$ happen concurrently, then r becomes quiescent, and then $<r:read(2), B>$ happens. The total order that explains this can be:<br/>
$<r:write(1), B>$, $<r:read(1), A>$, $<r:write(2), C>$, $<r:read(2), B>$

The total order makes sense, and we keep the Quiescent consistency rules as $<r:read(2), B>$ happens after all other events.

**Linearizability** - $<r:read(1), A>$, $<r:write(1), B>$, $<r:write(2), C>$ happen concurrently, so we can order the events the same way - $<r:write(1), B>$, $<r:read(1), A>$, $<r:write(2), C>$, $<r:read(2), B>$

We satisfy the linearization rules as:
- All operations took effect instantaneously at some point between invocation and response,
- The resulting order is a valid sequential history, and
- The real time order is kept - $<r:read(2), B>$ still scheduled after all other events in the suggested total order.

**Sequential consistency** - It follows that the history shown in Fig 3.13 is also sequentially consistent.
